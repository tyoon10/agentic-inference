## AI Week in Review: July 1, 2026
*Digest date range: Jun 24 – Jul 1, 2026*

---

### **Announcements: What Changed**

1. **OpenAI and Broadcom unveil Jalapeño, an LLM-optimized inference chip**
   *Jun 24, 2026*
   OpenAI and Broadcom introduced **Jalapeño**, a custom AI accelerator designed from the ground up for LLM inference. Early tests show significant performance-per-watt gains over state-of-the-art, with deployment slated for gigawatt-scale data centers in 2026. This deepens OpenAI’s full-stack control across models, systems, and silicon.
   [OpenAI][1]

2. **HP Inc. launches Frontier strategic partnership with OpenAI**
   *Jun 28, 2026*
   HP and OpenAI expanded their collaboration to embed AI across customer experiences, software development, and enterprise operations, marking one of OpenAI’s largest enterprise deployments to date.
   [OpenAI][1]

3. **Google DeepMind and A24 announce first-of-its-kind research partnership**
   *Jul 3, 2026*
   Google DeepMind and A24 are partnering to explore AI’s creative and narrative applications in entertainment, combining research expertise with cinematic storytelling.
   [DeepMind][2]

4. **Hugging Face and Cerebras bring Gemma 4 to real-time voice AI**
   *Jul 1, 2026*
   The integration enables 212-realtime voice interactions over WebSocket, delivering speech-to-speech inference using Gemma 4 for conversational AI.
   [Hugging Face][3]

5. **NVIDIA unlocks AI compute at scale with new cloud partnership model**
   *Jul 1, 2026*
   NVIDIA introduced a revenue-sharing model to accelerate deployment of large-scale AI factories, enabling rapid access to GB300-based infrastructure for AI-native startups and enterprises.
   [NVIDIA][4]

6. **Google DeepMind introduces computer use in Gemini 3.5 Flash**
   *Jun 24, 2026*
   Gemini 3.5 Flash now supports computer use, extending multimodal understanding for workflow automation and desktop-integrated AI applications.
   [DeepMind][2]

7. **Anthropic’s Claude Science integrated with NVIDIA BioNeMo for life sciences**
   *Jun 30, 2026*
   NVIDIA’s BioNeMo Agent Toolkit now powers **Claude Science**, enabling researchers to run accelerated AI workflows for drug discovery and computational biology.
   [NVIDIA][4]


---


### **Insight & Analysis: What It Means**

8. **LLMs are stuck in a groupthink groove—can innovation break through?**
   *Jul 1, 2026*
   A new startup, **Springboards**, argues that today’s LLMs converge on predictable outputs due to similar training regimes and data. Their model, *Flint*, is engineered to return high-variance responses on creative and open-ended prompts—challenging the “average answer” phenomenon noted across major chatbots.
   [MIT Technology Review][5]

9. **AMIE study in *Nature*: Can AI match doctors in disease management?**
   *Jun 16, 2026*
   Google’s **AMIE** research system matched primary care physicians in long-term disease management and outperformed them in plan preciseness and guideline alignment. The blinded study signals a shift from one-off diagnostics to multi-visit, guideline-driven care coordination—a potential inflection point for AI in healthcare workflows.
   [Google DeepMind][2]

10. **Achieving operational excellence with AI**
    *Jul 2, 2026*
    MIT Technology Review Insights, in partnership with Teleperformance, argues that companies with mature process frameworks (Lean Six Sigma, BPM) are best positioned to leverage AI. Process discipline turns AI into measurable value; otherwise, tools may under-deliver. The \$113B AI-driven process optimization market could reshape enterprise operations over the next decade.
    [MIT Technology Review Insights][6]

11. **Operator to “agentor”: Where generative AI is going at NVIDIA**
    *Jun 23, 2026*
    Telecommunications operators are moving from task automation to 24/7 AI agents for network and customer operations—signaling a broader pivot from reactive to autonomous infrastructure management across industries.
    [NVIDIA][4]
